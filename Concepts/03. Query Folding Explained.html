The Restaurant Analogy

Imagine you're at a huge restaurant with a massive menu (your raw data in a database).

You, the Customer = Power BI

You have a very specific order: "I want a hot cheeseburger, but only with pickles and ketchup, cut into four squares, and served on a blue plate."

The Waiter = Power Query

Your intermediary who takes your order and communicates with the kitchen.

The Kitchen = Database Server

A powerful, specialized space with all the equipment needed to prepare food efficiently.

How Query Folding Works

There are two ways the restaurant could handle your order:

Method 1: No Query Folding (The Inefficient Way)

1
Waiter brings you THE ENTIRE KITCHEN (all raw data)
↓
2
Sets up a tiny kitchen table on your lap (your local PC)
↓
3
You do all the work locally: butcher, cook, assemble, cut, plate
↓
4
Result: Slow, messy, and inefficient

Method 2: With Query Folding (The Efficient Way)

1
Waiter writes your order on a ticket (SQL query)
↓
2
Gives the ticket to the head chef in the kitchen (database server)
↓
3
Chef does all the work in the kitchen with proper equipment
↓
4
Waiter brings you the final, perfect burger (processed data)

Best Practices for Query Folding

To maximize query folding in Power BI, follow these guidelines:

Filter early: Apply filters as early as possible in your Power Query steps
Remove unnecessary columns: Select only the columns you need early in the process
Merge queries strategically: Merges (joins) between tables from the same source often fold
Avoid complex transformations early: Some operations (like extracting text) break folding
Check folding status: In Power Query, right-click a step → check if "View Native Query" is enabled
Use database views: Push complex logic to database views when possible

Key Takeaway

The Power BI Secret Sauce

Query Folding is Power BI's optimization technique that pushes data transformation work back to the powerful database server instead of bogging down your local computer.

Think of it as having a professional chef prepare your meal in a fully-equipped kitchen rather than trying to cook it yourself at the dining table with limited tools.

Result:

  • Faster reports and dashboards
  • Lower memory usage on your PC
  • Better performance with large datasets
  • Efficient use of database resources
